## More information about matrices

#### Order of matrices

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3      \\
2 & n.. & n..  \\
3 & n.. & n..
\end{bmatrix}
$$

order of matrix is given by $$m \times n$$. where $$m$$ is number of **rows** and $$n$$ is number of **columns**. for above matrix it is $$3 \times 3 = 9$$ as $$m,n=3$$.

> Two matrices are considered equal if both have same order and there components are same

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix} =
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 \\
3 & 4
\end{bmatrix}
$$

#### Types of matrices

> Column

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 \\
2 \\
3 \\
4 \\
5 \\
6 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

> Row

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2 & 3 & 4 & 5 & 6
\end{bmatrix}
$$

> square

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 2   & 3   & 4   & 5   & 6    \\
2 & n.. & n.. & n.. & n.. & n..  \\
3 & n.. & n.. & n.. & n.. & n..  \\
4 & n.. & n.. & n.. & n.. & n..  \\
5 & n.. & n.. & n.. & n.. & n..  \\
6 & n.. & n.. & n.. & n.. & n..  \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

> Diagonal

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 2 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 3 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 4 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 6  \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

*In this type of Matrix everything accept diagonal values are zero*

> Scalar Matrix

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
5 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 5 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 5 & 0 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 5 & 0 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 5 & 0  \\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 5  \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$

*Diagonal Matrix with every diagonal value being same*

> **Identify Matrix**

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 	\\
0 & 1 	\\
\end{bmatrix}
\Huge , \small
\begin{bmatrix}
1 & 0 & 0	\\
0 & 1 & 0	\\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{bmatrix}
$$

*Scalar Matrix with every diagonal value being 1*
Identify Matrix has use in Transformations it's basics building block in many mathematical systems

> Zero

$$
\begin{bmatrix}
0 & 0 & 0	\\
0 & 0 & 0	\\
0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
$$
